History of Tauste

A journey through the origins of Tauste, its evolution over the centuries and the development of its architectural, cultural and social heritage.

Origins and Middle Ages

Tauste has ancient origins, linked to settlements that took advantage of the proximity to the river and the trade routes of the time. During the Middle Ages, the town experienced notable growth around the Church of Santa María and the current Main Square.

The Mudejar style, characteristic of Aragon, was deeply reflected in the local architecture.

16th to 19th Centuries

Agriculture, trade and livestock were the pillars of Tauste's economic development in the Modern Age.

During this period, many of the traditions and festivities that are still alive today were consolidated.

Heritage and Cultural Legacy

The municipality preserves artistic and architectural elements of great interest, highlighting:

  • The tower of the Church of Santa María (Mudejar).
  • The historic urban layout around the Main Square.
  • Traditional civil buildings of the region.
